## Pattern 1: Lattice Projections as Observer Boundaries

**Observation**: Different observers correspond to projection maps onto sublattices.

**Hypothesis**: Observer topology (SYSCO-001) emerges from lattice structure.

**Implication**: Observer theory (SYSCO-005) may be derivable from lattices alone.

## Pattern 2: Confluence as No Hidden Symmetry Breaking

**Observation**: Confluence holds because rules are symmetric and non-interfering.

**Hypothesis**: Confluence is a fundamental consequence of symmetry.

**Implication**: **"Computation respects no hidden direction."**

**Observation**: Fixed points are the most informative consistent states.

**Hypothesis**: Fixed points are maximal elements in reachable state sets.

**Implication**: Connections to lattice theory and ultrafilters.

## Pattern 5: Reversibility without Explicit Storage

**Observation**: Reversibility might be computable on-the-fly without storing history.

**Hypothesis**: Lattice structure enables efficient inverse computation.

**Implication**: Practical reversible computation (SYSCO-008).

## Pattern 6: Emergence of Causality from Lattice Order

**Observation**: Lattice order induces causal structure despite no explicit time.

**Hypothesis**: Temporal causality is an artifact of partial observer knowledge.

**Implication**: Connection to relativistic causality (SYSCO-009).

## Pattern 7: Membranes as Natural Boundaries

**Observation**: Membranes emerge as minimal separations maintaining confluence.

**Hypothesis**: Membranes arise from information-locality principle.

**Implication**: Spatial structure is derived, not fundamental (SYSCO-004).

## Pattern 8: Decidability from Finiteness and Confluence

**Observation**: Decidability requires finiteness + termination + confluence.

**Hypothesis**: These form sufficiency conditions for decidability.

**Implication**: Generalizes beyond constraint lattices.
